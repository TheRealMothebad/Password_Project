What do I check for?

Any email could be malicious! Warning signs for malicious emails include:

  • Mispelled email addresses
  • Files to download
  • Links to click
  • Requests for information

Do not click suspicious links or open suspicious files.

Make sure that any information that you send electronically would be something you would be OK posting.

What information should you share on social media?

This is inherently a personal question. From a cybersecurity standpoint, the less information the better.

Think twice before posting:

  • Your name
  • Your birthday
  • Your location (past, present, and future)

    • This information could be used to impersonate you in a phishing attack. The safest strategy is to be completely anonymous.

Account settings can be used to further enhance privicy.

Rejecting use of an advertising ID makes tracking you around the web more difficult.

Also see if you can use social media settings to hide:

  • Your name
  • Your birthday
  • Your location

    • You can protect your friends privacy by not allowing social media platforms access to your contacts.

The cryptographic strength of a password is useless if you don't keep it a secret. A good password is only as strong as you are.

When making a password

Use:

  • Capitals
  • Numbers
  • Special characters
  • Multiple words

Do not use:

  • Personal information like:
    • Phone numbers
    • Pet names
    • Birthdays
    • addresses
  • Easy to guess words like:
    • Password
    • Hello
    • admin
    • letmein
